Key Findings — Presentation Notes

Source: simulation engine · BLS-calibrated wages
  • GDP grows ~75% over 20 years driven by AI productivity, despite higher unemployment.
  • Wage bifurcation. L1 (basic) loses ~15%; L5 (expert) gains ~22%.
  • Gini index rises from 0.41 → 0.43 in the moderate scenario.
  • Consumer spending dampens. Higher unemployment shrinks the spending ratio of GDP.
  • Productivity is the engine. 85% productivity boost at full adoption (adoption^0.7 × 0.85).
  • Output-employment decoupling is the central macroeconomic story.
